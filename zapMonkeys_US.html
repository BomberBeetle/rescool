Conservation comitee helps with the survival of Howler Monkeys

The animal conservation comitee, called Stop the Shocks, who were situated at the international base of the International Animal Rescue, in cooperation with the ICE, the local power agency, insulated 14 transformers in the town of Nosara, along with various cables, also trimming trees that would give the monkeys access to dangerous places.

They also put up a rope bridge in a place where the monkeys could access dangerous places. The comitee spent roughly $6000 to do that, an 21 more transformers will de insulated shortly, with $2500 from an anonymous donor.
